Welcome! I am Byeong-jun Park, an educational innovator and dedicated Social Studies teacher with 9 years of public school teaching experience in Gyeongsangnam-do, South Korea.

My pedagogical philosophy lives at the intersection of humanistic social studies education and digital technology. I believe the key to elevating humanity in the tech era lies in a fundamental question: on what grounds, from where, and how does human agency emerge during human-AI-digital orchestration? In both my classrooms and professional learning communities, I investigate how digital orchestrations can serve as tools for cognitive and social empowerment rather than passive consumption.

To address these challenges, my academic research agenda focuses on reforming AI-Education through formative Assessment & Feedback models and student-centered Learning Experience Design (LXD). At the same time, I am deeply committed to resolving the Digital Divide by leading grassroots educational initiatives that bring digital and AI literacy to underserved rural regions.

As the founder of the Gyeongsang Digital Educators Alliance (G-DEAL), I lead a network of over 500 educators. Together, we conduct digital volunteering, design specialized teacher professional development programs, and partner with EdTech enterprises. My ultimate goal is to nurture supportive, technology-infused learning environments that amplify, rather than replace, the human connection and agency in education.

Academic Foundations & Research

My pedagogical practices are anchored in solid instructional design theories. I hold a Master’s Degree in Educational Technology from Korea National University of Education (KNUE), where my research on feedback agency earned the Excellent Thesis Award under the supervision of Prof. Youngju Lee. Complementing my research, I serve as the Head of the Academic Division at the Gyeongsangnam-do Office of Education, designing professional development to build teachers' field-research capabilities and leading collaborative studies on policy trends and appropriate AI integration. Pushing the boundaries of digital education, I am currently preparing to apply for a Ph.D. program.

Current Research Questions & Agenda

My academic research centers on evaluating the pedagogical efficacy and agency effects of AI-generated feedback in secondary humanities education, addressing the following core inquiries:

Q1

AI-Based Feedback Acceptance & Engagement

Do learners genuinely accept AI-generated feedback and apply it as a concrete spark for revision? If not, what cognitive or psychological barriers prevent them from doing so?

Q2

Feedback Literacy & Agentic Scaffolding

How can we design instructional scaffolding and user experiences that enable secondary students to critically evaluate, adapt, and proactively adopt AI-driven feedback?

Q3

Cognitive Residue via AI Interaction

How can we engineer student-AI dialogue interactions to ensure learners retain "cognitive residue" (concept integration and memory) rather than offloading all thinking to AI agents?

Q4

Impact on Learner Agency & Metacognition

How do these conversational AI feedback architectures influence secondary school students' overall agency, self-directed learning models, and meta-learning capacities?
